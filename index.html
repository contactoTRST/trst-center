The Problem

Seeing is no longer believing.

For centuries, visual evidence was the gold standard. Today, a convincing deepfake video costs nothing and takes minutes to create. AI-generated images are indistinguishable to the human eye. Synthetic audio can clone anyone's voice from a 3-second clip.

The challenge is no longer whether fake content exists. The challenge is how you evaluate authenticity when you can't trust what you see — and the stakes are a published story, a court case, or an election.

How TRST Works

Six layers of evidence.
One clear verdict.

TRST doesn't guess. It moves through a decision tree — each layer is a gate. If the evidence is conclusive early, we stop. If not, we go deeper.

Layer 1
C2PA Provenance
Checks for a cryptographic certificate embedded at the moment of capture. If valid, the chain of custody is intact and origin is confirmed.
✓ If verified — analysis complete
Layer 2
Metadata Forensics
Reads EXIF data — camera model, GPS, timestamp, software field. Stripped or inconsistent metadata is a strong risk indicator.
Layer 3
Origin Tracing
Reverse image and video search to find where this content first appeared online, who published it, and when. Misattribution is a form of misinformation.
Layer 4
Pixel & Signal Analysis
Runs the media through leading AI detection APIs that identify GAN fingerprints, compression anomalies, facial inconsistencies, and generation artifacts.
Layer 5
LLM Consensus
Multiple AI models independently reason over the evidence collected. Not a guess — a jury. All three must agree before high confidence is reported.
Layer 6
Context & Credibility
Evaluates source reputation, publication history, and surrounding context. A real image used deceptively is still misinformation.

The Output

A report you can actually use.

Not a score you have to explain. A professional document with evidence — shareable, exportable as PDF, and citable in editorial decisions, legal filings, and investigations.
